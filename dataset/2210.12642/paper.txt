::: ltx_abstract
###### Abstract {#abstract .ltx_title .ltx_title_abstract}

Laplace approximation (LA) and its linearized variant (LLA) enable
effortless adaptation of pretrained deep neural networks to Bayesian
neural networks. The generalized Gauss-Newton (GGN) approximation is
typically introduced to improve their tractability. However, LA and LLA
are still confronted with non-trivial inefficiency issues and should
rely on Kronecker-factored, diagonal, or even last-layer approximate GGN
matrices in practical use. These approximations are likely to harm the
fidelity of learning outcomes. To tackle this issue, inspired by the
connections between LLA and neural tangent kernels (NTKs), we develop a
Nyström approximation to NTKs to accelerate LLA. Our method benefits
from the capability of popular deep learning libraries for forward mode
automatic differentiation, and enjoys reassuring theoretical guarantees.
Extensive studies reflect the merits of the proposed method in aspects
of both scalability and performance. Our method can even scale up to
architectures like vision transformers. We also offer valuable ablation
studies to diagnose our method. Code is available at
[https://github.com/thudzj/ELLA](https://github.com/thudzj/ELLA){.ltx_ref
.ltx_url .ltx_font_typewriter target="_blank"}.
:::

::: {#S1 .section .ltx_section}
## [1 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_section}Introduction {#introduction .ltx_title .ltx_title_section}

::: {#S1.p1 .ltx_para}
Deep neural networks (DNNs) excel at modeling deterministic
relationships and have become *de facto* solutions for diverse pattern
recognition
problems \[[18](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib18){.ltx_ref},
[54](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib54){.ltx_ref}\].
However, DNNs fall short in reasoning about model
uncertainty \[[1](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib1){.ltx_ref}\]
and suffer from poor
calibration \[[17](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib17){.ltx_ref}\].
These issues are intolerable in risk-sensitive scenarios like
self-driving \[[27](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib27){.ltx_ref}\],
healthcare \[[34](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib34){.ltx_ref}\],
finance \[[25](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib25){.ltx_ref}\],
etc.
:::

::: {#S1.p2 .ltx_para}
Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) have emerged as effective prescriptions
to these
pathologies \[[37](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib37){.ltx_ref},
[21](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib21){.ltx_ref},
[44](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib44){.ltx_ref},
[16](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib16){.ltx_ref}\].
They usually proceed by estimating the posteriors over high-dimensional
NN parameters. Due to some intractable integrals, diverse approximate
inference methods have been applied to learning BNNs, spanning
variational inference
(VI) \[[1](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib1){.ltx_ref},
[36](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib36){.ltx_ref}\],
Markov chain Monte Carlo
(MCMC) \[[55](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib55){.ltx_ref},
[4](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib4){.ltx_ref}\],
Laplace approximation
(LA) \[[38](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib38){.ltx_ref},
[50](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib50){.ltx_ref},
[30](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib30){.ltx_ref}\],
etc.
:::

::: {#S1.p3 .ltx_para}
LA has recently gained unprecedented attention because its *post-hoc*
nature nicely suits with the *pretraining-finetuning* fashion in deep
learning (DL). LA approximates the posterior with a Gaussian around its
maximum, whose mean and covariance are the *maximum a posteriori* (MAP)
and the inversion of the Hessian respectively. It is a common practice
to approximate the Hessian with the generalized Gauss-Newton (GGN)
matrix \[[40](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib40){.ltx_ref}\]
to make the whole workflow more tractable.
:::

::: {#S1.p4 .ltx_para}
Linearized LA
(LLA) \[[14](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib14){.ltx_ref},
[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref}\]
applies LA to the first-order approximation of the NN of concern. Immer
et
al. \[[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref}\]
argue that LLA is more sensible than LA in the presence of GGN
approximation; LLA can perform on par with or better than popular
alternatives on various uncertainty quantification (UQ)
tasks \[[14](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib14){.ltx_ref},
[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref},
[7](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib7){.ltx_ref}\].
The [Laplace]{#S1.p4.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_typewriter}
library \[[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref}\]
further substantially advances LLA's applicability, making it a simple
and competing baseline for Bayesian DL.
:::

::: {#S1.p5 .ltx_para}
The practical adoption of LA and LLA actually entails further
approximations on top of GGN. E.g., when using [Laplace]{#S1.p5.1.1
.ltx_text .ltx_font_typewriter} to process a pretrained
ResNet \[[18](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib18){.ltx_ref}\],
practitioners are recommended to resort to a Kronecker-factored
(KFAC) \[[40](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib40){.ltx_ref}\]
or diagonal approximation of the full GGN matrix for tractability. An
orthogonal tactic is to apply LA/LLA to only NNs' last
layer \[[30](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib30){.ltx_ref}\].
Yet, the approximation errors in these cases can hardly be identified,
significantly undermining the fidelity of the learning outcomes.
:::

::: {#S1.p6 .ltx_para}
This paper aims at scaling LLA up to make probabilistic predictions in a
more assurable way. We first revisit the inherent connections between
Neural Tangent Kernels
(NTKs) \[[24](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib24){.ltx_ref}\]
and
LLA \[[28](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib28){.ltx_ref},
[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref}\],
and find that, if we can approximate the NTKs with the inner product of
some low-dimensional vector representations of the data, LLA can be
considerably accelerated. Given this finding, we propose to adapt the
Nyström method to approximate the NTKs of multi-output NNs, and advocate
leveraging forward mode automatic differentiation (*fwAD*) to
efficiently compute the involved Jacobian-vector products (JVPs). The
resultant *accElerated LLA (ELLA)* preserves the principal structures of
vanilla LLA yet without explicitly computing/storing the costly
GGN/Jacobian matrices for the training data. What's more, we
theoretically analyze the approximation error between the predictive of
ELLA and that of vanilla LLA, and find that it deceases rapidly as the
Nyström approximation becomes accurate.
:::

::: {#S1.p7 .ltx_para}
We perform extensive studies to show that ELLA can be a low-cost and
effective baseline for Bayesian DL. We first describe how to specify the
hyperparameters of ELLA, and use an illustrative regression task to
demonstrate the effectiveness of ELLA (see [[Figure]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [1]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S1.F1 "Figure 1 ‣ 1 Introduction ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}).
We then experiment on standard image classification benchmarks to
exhibit the superiority of ELLA over competing baselines in aspects of
both performance and scalability. We further show that ELLA can even
scale up to modern architectures like vision transformers
(ViTs) \[[11](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib11){.ltx_ref}\].
:::

::: {#S2 .section .ltx_section}
## [2 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_section}Background {#background .ltx_title .ltx_title_section}

::: {#S2.p1 .ltx_para}
Consider a learning problem on
$\mathcal{D} = {(\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y})} = {\{{({\mathbf{x}}_{i},{\mathbf{y}}_{i})}\}}_{i = 1}^{N}$,
where ${\mathbf{x}}_{i} \in \mathcal{X}$ and
${\mathbf{y}}_{i} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{C}$ (e.g., regression) or
${\{ 0,1\}}^{C}$ (e.g., classification) refer to observations and
targets respectively. The advance in machine learning suggests using an
NN
${g_{\mathbf{θ}}\operatorname{}{( \cdot )}}:{\mathcal{X}\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}}^{C}}$
with parameters ${\mathbf{θ}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{P}$ for data fitting.
Despite well-performing, regularly trained NNs only capture the most
likely interpretation for the data, thus miss the ability to reason
about uncertainty and are prone to overfitting and overconfidence.
:::

::: {#S2.p2 .ltx_para}
BNNs \[[37](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib37){.ltx_ref},
[21](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib21){.ltx_ref},
[44](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib44){.ltx_ref}\]
characterize model uncertainty by probabilistic principle and can
holistically represent all likely interpretations. Typically, BNNs
impose a prior $p\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}$ on NN parameters and
chase the Bayesian posterior [[
[${p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{θ}} \middle| \mathcal{D} \right.)}} = {{{p\operatorname{}{(\left. \mathcal{D} \middle| {\mathbf{θ}} \right.)}\operatorname{}p\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}}/p}\operatorname{}{(\mathcal{D})}}$]{#S2.p2.2.m2.1.1.1.1
.ltx_p} ]{.ltx_transformed_inner
style="transform:translate(-2.7pt,0.2pt) scale(0.95,0.95) ;"}]{#S2.p2.2.m2.1.1.1
.ltx_inline-block .ltx_markedasmath .ltx_transformed_outer
style="width:103.9pt;height:9.5pt;vertical-align:-2.4pt;"} where [[
[${p\operatorname{}{(\left. \mathcal{D} \middle| {\mathbf{θ}} \right.)}} = {\prod_{i}{p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}}_{i} \middle| {{\mathbf{x}}_{i},{\mathbf{θ}}} \right.)}}} = {\prod_{i}{p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}}_{i} \middle| {g_{\mathbf{θ}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{i})}} \right.)}}}$]{#S2.p2.3.m3.1.1.1.1
.ltx_p} ]{.ltx_transformed_inner
style="transform:translate(-4.1pt,0.2pt) scale(0.95,0.95) ;"}]{#S2.p2.3.m3.1.1.1
.ltx_inline-block .ltx_markedasmath .ltx_transformed_outer
style="width:155.2pt;height:16.6pt;vertical-align:-9.5pt;"}. Analytical
estimation is usually intractable due to NNs' high nonlinearity.
Thereby, BNN methods usually find a surrogate of the true posterior
${q\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}} \approx {p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{θ}} \middle| \mathcal{D} \right.)}}$
via approximate inference methods like variational inference
(VI) \[[1](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib1){.ltx_ref},
[20](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib20){.ltx_ref},
[36](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib36){.ltx_ref},
[62](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib62){.ltx_ref},
[29](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib29){.ltx_ref}\],
Laplace approximation
(LA) \[[38](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib38){.ltx_ref},
[50](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib50){.ltx_ref}\],
Markov chain Monte Carlo
(MCMC) \[[55](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib55){.ltx_ref},
[4](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib4){.ltx_ref},
[63](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib63){.ltx_ref}\],
particle-optimization based variational inference
(POVI) \[[35](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib35){.ltx_ref}\],
etc.
:::

::: {#S2.p3 .ltx_para}
BNNs predict for new data ${\mathbf{x}}_{*}$ by posterior predictive
${p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {{\mathbf{x}}_{*},\mathcal{D}} \right.)}} = {{\mathbb{E}}_{p\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{θ}}|\mathcal{D}})}}\operatorname{}p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {{\mathbf{x}}_{*},{\mathbf{θ}}} \right.)}} \approx {{\mathbb{E}}_{q\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}}\operatorname{}p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {{\mathbf{x}}_{*},{\mathbf{θ}}} \right.)}} \approx {\frac{1}{S}\operatorname{}{\sum_{s = 1}^{S}{p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {g_{{\mathbf{θ}}_{s}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{*})}} \right.)}}}}$
where ${\mathbf{θ}}_{s} \sim {q\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}}$ are
i.i.d. Monte Carlo (MC) samples.
:::

::: {#S2.SS1 .section .ltx_subsection}
### [2.1 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}Laplace Approximation and Its Linearized Variant {#laplace-approximation-and-its-linearized-variant .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S2.SS1.p1 .ltx_para}
Typically, LA builds a Gaussian approximate posterior in the form of
${{q\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}} = {\mathcal{N}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}};\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{\Sigma})}}},$
where $\hat{\mathbf{θ}}$ denotes the MAP solution, i.e.,
$\hat{\mathbf{θ}} = {{{\arg{\max_{\mathbf{θ}}{\log p}}}\operatorname{}{(\left. \mathcal{D} \middle| {\mathbf{θ}} \right.)}} + {{\log p}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}}}$,
and $\mathbf{\Sigma}$ is the inversion of the Hessian of the negative
log posterior w.r.t. parameters, i.e.,
${\mathbf{\Sigma}^{- 1} = {- {{\nabla_{{\mathbf{θ}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{θ}}}^{2}{({{{\log p}\operatorname{}{(\left. \mathcal{D} \middle| {\mathbf{θ}} \right.)}} + {{\log p}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}}})}}|}_{{\mathbf{θ}} = \hat{\mathbf{θ}}}}}.$
Without loss of generality, we base the following discussion on the
isotropic Gaussian prior
${p\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}} = {\mathcal{N}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}};\mathbf{0},{\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\mathbf{I}_{P}})}}$[^0^[[^0^[0]{.ltx_tag
.ltx_tag_note}$\mathbf{I}_{P}$ refers to the identity matrix of size
$P \times P$.]{.ltx_note_content}]{.ltx_note_outer}]{#footnote0
.ltx_note .ltx_role_footnote}, so
$- {{{{\nabla_{{\mathbf{θ}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{θ}}}^{2}\log}p}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}}|}_{{\mathbf{θ}} = \hat{\mathbf{θ}}}$
equals to $\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}$.
:::

::: {#S2.SS1.p2 .ltx_para}
Due to the intractability of the Hessian for NNs with massive
parameters, it is a common practice to use the symmetric positive and
semi-definite (SPSD) GGN matrix as a workaround, i.e.,

  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${\mathbf{\Sigma}^{- 1} = {{\sum\limits_{i}{J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{i} \right)^{\top}\operatorname{}\Lambda\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{i},{\mathbf{y}}_{i} \right)\operatorname{}J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{i} \right)}} + \left. \mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right.}},$$      [(1)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------

where
${J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}} \triangleq {{{\nabla_{\mathbf{θ}}g_{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}}|}_{{\mathbf{θ}} = \hat{\mathbf{θ}}}$
and
${\Lambda\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{y}})}} \triangleq {- {{{{\nabla_{{\mathbf{g}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{g}}}^{2}\log}p}\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {\mathbf{g}} \right.)}}|}_{{\mathbf{g}} = {g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}}}}$.
:::

::: {#S2.SS1.p3 .ltx_para}
When concatenating
${\{{{J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{i})}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{C \times P}}\}}_{i = 1}^{N}$
as a big matrix
$\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{{N\operatorname{}C} \times P}$
and organizing
${\{{{\Lambda\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{i},{\mathbf{y}}_{i})}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{C \times C}}\}}_{i = 1}^{N}$
as a block-diagonal matrix
$\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{{{N\operatorname{}C} \times N}\operatorname{}C}$,
we have:

  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${\mathbf{\Sigma} = \left\lbrack {{{}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}} + \left. \mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right.} \right\rbrack^{- 1}}.$$      [(2)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------

Yet, LA suffers from
underfitting \[[33](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib33){.ltx_ref}\].
This is probably because GGN approximation implicitly turns the original
model $g_{\mathbf{θ}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}$ into a linear one
${g_{\mathbf{θ}}^{\text{lin}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}} = {{g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}} + {J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{θ}} - \hat{\mathbf{θ}}})}}}$
but $g_{\mathbf{θ}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}$ is still leveraged
to make prediction. I.e., there exists a *shift* between posterior
inference and
prediction \[[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref}\].
To mitigate this issue, a proposal is to predict with
$g_{\mathbf{θ}}^{\text{lin}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}$, giving
rise to linearized LA
(LLA) \[[14](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib14){.ltx_ref},
[28](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib28){.ltx_ref},
[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref}\].
:::

::: {#S2.SS1.p4 .ltx_para}
LA and LLA nicely fit the *pretraining-finetuning* fashion in DL -- they
can be *post-hoc* applied to pretrained models where only the GGN
matrices require to be estimated and further inverted. By LA and LLA,
practitioners can adapt off-the-shelf high performing DNNs to BNNs
easily.
:::

::: {#S2.SS1.p5 .ltx_para}
LLA has revealed strong results on diverse UQ
problems \[[14](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib14){.ltx_ref},
[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref},
[7](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib7){.ltx_ref}\].
The [Laplace]{#S2.SS1.p5.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_typewriter}
library \[[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref}\]
further advances LLA's applicability, and evidences LLA is competitive
to popular
alternatives \[[63](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib63){.ltx_ref},
[32](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib32){.ltx_ref},
[39](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib39){.ltx_ref}\].
:::

::: {#S2.SS1.p6 .ltx_para}
[Scalability issue]{#S2.SS1.p6.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold} The GGN
matrix of size $P \times P$ is still unamenable in modern DL scenarios,
so further approximations sparsifying it are always introduced. The
diagonal and
KFAC \[[40](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib40){.ltx_ref}\]
approximations are commonly adopted
ones \[[50](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib50){.ltx_ref},
[62](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib62){.ltx_ref}\],
where only a diagonal or block-diagonal structure of the original GNN
matrix is preserved. An orthogonal tactic is to concern only a subspace
of the high-dimensional parameter space (e.g., the parameter space of
the last
layer \[[30](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib30){.ltx_ref}\])
to reduce the scale of the GGN matrix. However, these strategies
sacrifice the fidelity of the learning outcomes as the approximation
errors in these cases can hardly be theoretically measured. To this end,
we develop *accElerated Linearized Laplace Approximation (ELLA)* to push
the limit of LLA in a more assurable way.
:::
:::
:::

::: {#S3 .section .ltx_section}
## [3 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_section}Methodology {#methodology .ltx_title .ltx_title_section}

::: {#S3.p1 .ltx_para}
In this section, we first revisit the relation of LLA to Gaussian
processes (GPs) and Neural Tangent Kernels
(NTKs) \[[24](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib24){.ltx_ref}\].
After that, we reveal how to accelerate LLA by kernel approximation.
Based on these findings, we develop an efficient implementation of ELLA
using the Nyström
method \[[58](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib58){.ltx_ref}\].
:::

::: {#S3.SS1 .section .ltx_subsection}
### [3.1 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}The Gaussian Process View of LLA {#the-gaussian-process-view-of-lla .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S3.SS1.p1 .ltx_para}
Integrating
${q\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}})}} = {\mathcal{N}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{θ}};\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{\Sigma})}}$
with the linear model
$g_{\mathbf{θ}}^{\text{lin}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}$ actually
gives rise to a function-space approximate
posterior \[[14](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib14){.ltx_ref},
[28](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib28){.ltx_ref},
[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref}\]
in the form of
${q\operatorname{}{(f)}} = {\mathcal{G}\operatorname{}\mathcal{P}\operatorname{}{(\left. f \middle| {{g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}},{\kappa_{\text{LLA}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}}} \right.)}}$
with

  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${{\kappa_{\text{LLA}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)} \triangleq {J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Sigma}\operatorname{}J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)^{\top}}}.$$      [(3)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
:::

::: {#S3.SS1.p2 .ltx_para}
By Woodbury matrix
identity \[[60](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib60){.ltx_ref}\],
we have:

  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${\mathbf{\Sigma} = \left\lbrack {{{}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}} + \left. \mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right.} \right\rbrack^{- 1} = {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{I}_{P} - {{}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {\left. \mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{- 1}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right. + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}{}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{}}} \right\rbrack^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}} \right)}}.$$      [(4)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------

It follows that

  -- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${{\kappa_{\text{LLA}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)} = {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\left( {{\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)} - {\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},\mathbf{X})\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {\left. \mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{- 1}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right. + {\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}(\mathbf{X},\mathbf{X})}} \right\rbrack^{- 1}\operatorname{}\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( \mathbf{X},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)}} \right)}},$$      [(5)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------

where
${\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}} \triangleq {J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}\operatorname{}J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}^{\top}}$
denotes the neural tangent kernel
(NTK) \[[24](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib24){.ltx_ref}\]
corresponding to $g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}$.
Note that
$\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}$
is a *matrix-valued* kernel, with values in the space of $C \times C$
matrices.
:::

::: {#S3.SS1.p3 .ltx_para}
The main challenge then turns into the computation and inversion of the
gram matrix
$\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}{(\mathbf{X},\mathbf{X})}$ of size
${{N\operatorname{}C} \times N}\operatorname{}C$. When either $N$ or $C$
is large, the estimation of $\kappa_{\text{LLA}}$ still suffers from
inefficiency issue. To address this, existing
work \[[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref}\]
assumes independence among the $C$ output dimensions to cast
$q\operatorname{}{(f)}$ into $C$ independent GPs following
\[[51](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib51){.ltx_ref}\],
and randomly subsample $M \ll N$ data points to form a cheap substitute
for the original gram matrix. Despite effective in some cases, these
approximations are heuristic, lacking a clear theoretical foundation.
:::
:::

::: {#S3.SS2 .section .ltx_subsection}
### [3.2 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}Scale Up LLA by Kernel Approximation {#scale-up-lla-by-kernel-approximation .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S3.SS2.p1 .ltx_para}
We show that if we can approximate
$\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}$
with the inner product of some explicit $C \times K$-dimensional
representations of the data, i.e.,
${\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}} \approx {\varphi\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}\operatorname{}\varphi\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}^{\top}}$
with $\varphi:{\mathcal{X}\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}}^{C \times K}}$, the
scalability of the whole workflow can be unleashed.
:::

::: {#S3.SS2.p2 .ltx_para}
Concretely, letting
${\mathbf{φ}}_{\mathbf{X}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{{N\operatorname{}C} \times K}$
be the concatenation of
${\{{{\varphi\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{i})}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{C \times K}}\}}_{i = 1}^{N}$,
we have (detailed derivation in [[A.2]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A1.SS2 "A.2 Derivation of Equation 6 ‣ Appendix A Proof ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref};
see also
\[[9](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib9){.ltx_ref}\])

  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ---------------------------------------------------------
     ${\kappa_{\text{LLA}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)} \approx$   $\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\left( {{\varphi\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})\operatorname{}\varphi\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)^{\top}} - {\varphi\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})\operatorname{}{\mathbf{φ}}_{\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {\left. \mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{- 1}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right. + {{\mathbf{φ}}_{\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{φ}}_{\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}} \right\rbrack^{- 1}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{φ}}_{\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\varphi\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)^{\top}}} \right)$      [(6)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equationgroup .ltx_align_right}
     $=$                                                                                               ${{\varphi\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})\operatorname{}\left\lbrack \underset{\mathbf{G}}{\underbrace{{\underset{i}{\sum}{\varphi\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{i} \right)^{\top}\Lambda\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{i},{\mathbf{y}}_{i} \right)\varphi\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{i} \right)}}+\left. \mathbf{I}_{K}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right.}} \right\rbrack^{- 1}\operatorname{}\varphi\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)^{\top}} \triangleq {\kappa_{\text{ELLA}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)}}.$                                                                         
  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ---------------------------------------------------------

The matrix $\mathbf{G} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{K \times K}$ possesses a
similar formula to the $\mathbf{\Sigma}$ in the original
$\kappa_{\text{LLA}}$ in [[Equation]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [3]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E3 "3 ‣ 3.1 The Gaussian Process View of LLA ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref},
yet much smaller. Once having $\varphi$, it is only required to perform
one forward pass of $g$ and $\varphi$ for each training data to estimate
$\mathbf{G}$. When $K$ is reasonably small, e.g., $< 100$, it is cheap
to invert $\mathbf{G}$.
:::
:::

::: {#S3.SS3 .section .ltx_subsection}
### [3.3 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}Approximate NTKs via the Nyström Method {#approximate-ntks-via-the-nyström-method .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S3.SS3.p1 .ltx_para}
Typical kernel approximation means include Nyström
method \[[45](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib45){.ltx_ref},
[58](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib58){.ltx_ref}\]
and random features-based
methods \[[48](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib48){.ltx_ref},
[49](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib49){.ltx_ref},
[61](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib61){.ltx_ref},
[43](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib43){.ltx_ref},
[15](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib15){.ltx_ref},
[9](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib9){.ltx_ref}\].
Given that the latter routinely relies on a relatively large number of
random features to gain a faithful approximation (which means a large
$K$), we suggest leveraging the Nyström method, which captures only
several principal components of the kernel, to build $\varphi$.
:::

::: {#S3.SS3.p2 .ltx_para}
To comfortably apply the Nyström method, we first rewrite
$\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}$
as a *scalar-valued* kernel
${\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( ({\mathbf{x}},i),\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime},i^{\prime} \right) \right)} = {J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)\operatorname{}J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime},i^{\prime} \right)^{\top}}$
where [[
[$J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}{({\mathbf{x}},i)} \triangleq \nabla_{\mathbf{θ}}\left\lbrack g_{\mathbf{θ}}{({\mathbf{x}})} \right\rbrack^{(i)}|_{{\mathbf{θ}} = \hat{\mathbf{θ}}}:\mathcal{X} \times {\lbrack C\rbrack}\rightarrow{\mathbb{R}}^{1 \times P}$]{#S3.SS3.p2.3.m3.1.1.1.1
.ltx_p} ]{.ltx_transformed_inner
style="transform:translate(-8.1pt,0.5pt) scale(0.9,0.9) ;"}]{#S3.SS3.p2.3.m3.1.1.1
.ltx_inline-block .ltx_markedasmath .ltx_transformed_outer
style="width:145.4pt;height:11.2pt;vertical-align:-2.6pt;"}[^1^[[^1^[1]{.ltx_tag
.ltx_tag_note}We use superscript to index a multi-dimensional tensor and
$\lbrack C\rbrack$ represents set of integers from $1$ to
$C$.]{.ltx_note_content}]{.ltx_note_outer}]{#footnote1 .ltx_note
.ltx_role_footnote} computes the gradient of $i$-th output w.r.t.
parameters.
:::

::: {#S3.SS3.p3 .ltx_para}
By Mercer's
theorem \[[42](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib42){.ltx_ref}\],

  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${{\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( ({\mathbf{x}},i),\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime},i^{\prime} \right) \right)} = {\sum\limits_{k \geq 1}{\mu_{k}\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime},i^{\prime} \right)}}},$$      [(7)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------

where
$\psi_{k} \in {L^{2}\operatorname{}{({\mathcal{X} \times {\lbrack C\rbrack}},q)}}$[^2^[[^2^[2]{.ltx_tag
.ltx_tag_note}$L^{2}{(,q)}$ denotes the space of square-integrable
functions w.r.t. $q$.]{.ltx_note_content}]{.ltx_note_outer}]{#footnote2
.ltx_note .ltx_role_footnote} refer to the eigenfunctions of the NTK
w.r.t. some probability measure $q$ with $\mu_{k} \geq 0$ as the
associated eigenvalues. Based on this, the Nyström method discovers the
top-$K$ eigenvalues as well as the corresponding eigenfunctions for
kernel approximation.
:::

::: {#S3.SS3.p4 .ltx_para}
By definition, the eigenfunctions can represent the spectral information
of the kernel:

  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${{\left. \int{\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( ({\mathbf{x}},i),\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime},i^{\prime} \right) \right)\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime},i^{\prime} \right)\operatorname{}q\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}^{\prime},i^{\prime} \right)} \right. = {\mu_{k}\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)}},{{\forall k} \geq 1}},$$      [(8)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------

while being orthonormal under $q$:

  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ -- ----------------------------------------------------
     $${{\left. \int{\psi_{k}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)\operatorname{}\psi_{k^{\prime}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)\operatorname{}q\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)} \right. = {{\mathbb{1}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {k = k^{\prime}} \right\rbrack},{\forall k}}},{k^{\prime} \geq 1}}.$$      [(9)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ -- ----------------------------------------------------

In our case, $q\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}$ can be factorized as
the product of the data distribution and a uniform distribution over
$\{ 1,\ldots,C\}$, which can be trivially sampled from. The Nyström
method draws $M$ ($M \geq K$) i.i.d. samples
$\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}} = {\{{({\mathbf{x}}_{1},i_{1})},\ldots,{({\mathbf{x}}_{M},i_{M})}\}}$
from $q$ to approximate the integration in [[Equation]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [8]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E8 "8 ‣ 3.3 Approximate NTKs via the Nyström Method ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}:

  -- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------
     $${{{\frac{1}{M}\operatorname{}{\sum\limits_{m = 1}^{M}{\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( ({\mathbf{x}},i),\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{m},i_{m} \right) \right)\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{m},i_{m} \right)}}} = {\mu_{k}\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)}},{{\forall k} \in \lbrack K\rbrack}}.$$      [(10)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------

Applying this equation to these samples gives rise to:

  -- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------
     $${{{\frac{1}{M}\operatorname{}{\sum\limits_{m = 1}^{M}{\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( \left( {\mathbf{x}}_{m^{\prime}},i_{m^{\prime}} \right),\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{m},i_{m} \right) \right)\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{m},i_{m} \right)}}} = {\mu_{k}\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{m^{\prime}},i_{m^{\prime}} \right)}},{{{\forall k} \in \lbrack K\rbrack},{m^{\prime} \in \lbrack M\rbrack}}},$$      [(11)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------

then we arrive at

  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------
     $${{{\frac{1}{M}\operatorname{}\mathbf{K}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{ψ}}_{k}} \approx {\mu_{k}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{ψ}}_{k}}},{k \in \lbrack K\rbrack}},$$      [(12)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------

where
$\mathbf{K} = {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}}$,
with
$\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{M \times P}$
as the concatenation of
${\{{{J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{m},i_{m})}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{1 \times P}}\}}_{m = 1}^{M}$,
and
${\mathbf{ψ}}_{k} = {\lbrack{\psi_{k}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{1},i_{1})}},\ldots,{\psi_{k}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{M},i_{M})}}\rbrack}^{\top}$.
This implies a scaled eigendecomposition problem of $\mathbf{K}$.
:::

::: {#S3.SS3.p5 .ltx_para}
We compute the top-$K$ eigenvalues $\lambda_{1},\ldots,\lambda_{K}$ of
matrix $\mathbf{K}$ and record the corresponding *orthonormal*
eigenvectors ${\mathbf{u}}_{1},\ldots,{\mathbf{u}}_{K}$. Given the
constraint in [[Equation]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [9]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E9 "9 ‣ 3.3 Approximate NTKs via the Nyström Method ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref},
it is easy to see that:

  -- ----------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------------
     $\mu_{k}$   ${{\approx \frac{\lambda_{k}}{M}},{{{\text{and}\operatorname{}\psi_{k}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{m},i_{m})}} \approx {\sqrt{M}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{u}}_{k}^{(m)}}},{m \in {\lbrack M\rbrack}}}}.$      [(13)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equationgroup .ltx_align_right}
  -- ----------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------------

Combining [[Equation]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [13]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E13 "13 ‣ 3.3 Approximate NTKs via the Nyström Method ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}
and ([[10]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E10 "10 ‣ 3.3 Approximate NTKs via the Nyström Method ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref})
yields the Nyström approximation of the top-$K$ eigenfunctions:

  -- --------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------------
     ${\hat{\psi}}_{k}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}},i)$   ${= {\frac{\sqrt{M}}{\lambda_{k}}\operatorname{}{\sum\limits_{m = 1}^{M}{{\mathbf{u}}_{k}^{(m)}\operatorname{}\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}\left( {({\mathbf{x}},i)},{({\mathbf{x}}_{m},i_{m})} \right)}}} = {\frac{\sqrt{M}}{\lambda_{k}}\operatorname{}J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{u}}_{k}}}.$      [(14)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equationgroup .ltx_align_right}
  -- --------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- ----------------------------------------------------------

Then, the mapping $\varphi$ which satisfied
${\kappa_{\text{NTK}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}} \approx {\varphi\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}\operatorname{}\varphi\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}^{\top}}$
can be realized as:

  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------
     $\left\lbrack {\varphi\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})} \right\rbrack^{(i,k)}$   ${= {{\hat{\psi}}_{k}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}\operatorname{}\sqrt{\mu_{k}}} = {{J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{u}}_{k}}/\sqrt{\lambda_{k}}}},$                                                                                                             [(15)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
     $\Rightarrow{\varphi\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}}$                         ${= {{\lbrack{J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{v}}_{1}},\ldots,{J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{v}}_{K}}\rbrack}\operatorname{}\text{with}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{v}}_{k}} = {{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{u}}_{k}}/\sqrt{\lambda_{k}}}}.$      [(16)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------

[Connection to sparse approximations of GPs]{#S3.SS3.p5.8.1 .ltx_text
.ltx_font_bold} A recent
work \[[57](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib57){.ltx_ref}\]
has shown that sparse variational Gaussian processes
(SVGP) \[[53](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib53){.ltx_ref},
[3](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib3){.ltx_ref}\]
is algebraically equivalent to the Nyström approximation because the
Evidence Lower BOund (ELBO) of the former encompasses the learning
objective of the latter. That's to say, ELLA implicitly deploys a sparse
approximation to the original GP predictive of LLA.
:::

::: {#S3.SS3.p6 .ltx_para}
[Discussion]{#S3.SS3.p6.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold} In this work, the
developed Nyström approximation to NTKs is mainly used for accelerating
the computation of the predictive covariance of LLA, while it can also
be easily applied to future work on the practical application of NTKs.
:::
:::

::: {#S3.SS4 .section .ltx_subsection}
### [3.4 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}Implementation {#implementation .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S3.SS4.p1 .ltx_para}
As shown, the estimation of $\varphi$ on a data point $\mathbf{x}$
degenerates as $K$ JVPs, which can be accomplished by invoking *fwAD*
for $K$ times:

  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------
     $${{\left( {\mathbf{x}},\begin{array}{cl}                                                                [(17)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
      & \hat{\mathbf{θ}} \\                                                                                   
      & {\mathbf{v}}_{k}                                                                                      
     \end{array} \right)\overset{\text{forward}}{\overset{\mspace{102mu}}{\rightarrow}}\begin{array}{cl}      
      & {g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})} \\                                                
      & {J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})\operatorname{}{\mathbf{v}}_{k}}                    
     \end{array}},{k \in \lbrack K\rbrack}},$$                                                                
  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------

where the model output
$g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}$ and the JVP
$J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}})}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{v}}_{k}$
are simultaneously computed in *one single forward pass*. Prevalent DL
libraries like
PyTorch \[[47](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib47){.ltx_ref}\]
and
Jax \[[2](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib2){.ltx_ref}\]
have already been armed with the capability for *fwAD*.
Algorithm [[2]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#algorithm2 "2 ‣ 3.4 Implementation ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}
details the procedure of building $\varphi$ in a PyTorch-like style.
:::

::: {#S3.SS4.p2 .ltx_para}
With that, we can trivially compute the posterior
$q\operatorname{}{(f)}$ according to [[Equation]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [6]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E6 "6 ‣ 3.2 Scale Up LLA by Kernel Approximation ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref},
as detailed in Algorithm [[1]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#algorithm1 "1 ‣ 3.4 Implementation ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}.
The procedures for estimating $\mathbf{G}$ and the posterior can both be
batched for acceleration.
:::

[Computation overhead]{#S3.SS4.p3.12.3 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold} The
estimation of $\varphi$ involves $M$ forward and backward passes of
$g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}$ and the eigendecomposition of a matrix of size
$M \times M$. After that, we only need to make
${\{{\mathbf{v}}_{k}\}}_{k = 1}^{K}$ persistent, which amounts to
*storing $K$ more NN copies*. Estimating $\mathbf{G}^{- 1}$ requires
scanning the training set once and inverting a matrix of $K \times K$,
which is cheap. The evaluation of $q\operatorname{}{(f)}$ embodies that
of $\varphi$, i.e., performing *$K$ forward passes under the scope of*
*fwAD*. This is similar to other BNNs that perform $S$ forward passes
with different MC parameter samples to estimate the posterior
predictive.

::: {#S4 .section .ltx_section}
## [4 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_section}Theoretical Analysis {#theoretical-analysis .ltx_title .ltx_title_section}

::: {#S4.p1 .ltx_para}
In this section, we theoretically analyze the approximation error of
$\kappa_{\text{ELLA}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}$
to
$\kappa_{\text{LLA}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}$.
:::

::: {#S4.p2 .ltx_para}
To reduce unnecessary complexity, this section assumes using $M = K$
i.i.d. MC samples when performing the Nyström method. Then,
$\kappa_{\text{ELLA}}$ can be reformulated as (details deferred to
[[A.3]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A1.SS3 "A.3 Derivation of Equation 18 ‣ Appendix A Proof ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref})

  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------
     $${{\kappa_{\text{ELLA}}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime} \right)} = {J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})\operatorname{}\underset{\mathbf{\Sigma}^{\prime}}{\underbrace{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\left\lbrack {{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}}+\left. {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right.} \right\rbrack^{-1}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}}}\operatorname{}J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}({\mathbf{x}})^{\top}}}.$$      [(18)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------

Thereby, the gap between
$\kappa_{\text{ELLA}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}$
and
$\kappa_{\text{LLA}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},{\mathbf{x}}^{\prime})}$
can be upper bounded by
$\mathcal{E} = {\|{\mathbf{\Sigma}^{\prime} - \mathbf{\Sigma}}\|}$,
where $\parallel \cdot \parallel$ represents the matrix 2-norm (i.e.,
the spectral norm). In typical cases, $P \gg M$, thus with high
probability
$\mathbf{K} = {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}} \succ 0$.
We present an upper bound of $\mathcal{E}$ as follows.
:::

::: {#Thmthm1 .ltx_theorem .ltx_theorem_thm}
###### [[Theorem 1]{#Thmthm1.1.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold}]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_theorem}[ ]{#Thmthm1.2.2 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold}(Proof in [[A.4]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A1.SS4 "A.4 Proof of Theorem 1 ‣ Appendix A Proof ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref})[.]{#Thmthm1.3.3 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold} {#theorem-1-proof-in-a.4. .ltx_title .ltx_runin .ltx_title_theorem}

::: {#Thmthm1.p1 .ltx_para}
[Let $c_{\Lambda}$ be a finite constant associated with $\Lambda$, and
$\mathcal{E}^{\prime}$ the error of Nyström approximation [[
[$\|{{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}})}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}}\|$]{#Thmthm1.p1.4.4.m4.1.1.1.1
.ltx_p} ]{.ltx_transformed_inner
style="transform:translate(-7.5pt,0.5pt) scale(0.9,0.9) ;"}]{#Thmthm1.p1.4.4.m4.1.1.1
.ltx_inline-block .ltx_markedasmath .ltx_transformed_outer
style="width:134.5pt;height:12pt;vertical-align:-3.6pt;"}. It holds
that]{#Thmthm1.p1.4.4 .ltx_text .ltx_font_italic}

  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ --
     ${\mathcal{E} \leq {{\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}c_{\Lambda}\operatorname{}\mathcal{E}^{\prime}} + \sigma_{0}^{2}}}.$   
  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ --
:::
:::

::: {#S4.p3 .ltx_para}
$\mathcal{E}^{\prime}$ has been extensively analyzed by pioneering
work \[[12](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib12){.ltx_ref},
[5](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib5){.ltx_ref},
[26](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib26){.ltx_ref}\],
and we simply adapt the results developed by Drineas and
Mahoney \[[12](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib12){.ltx_ref}\]
to our case. We denote the maximum diagonal entry and the $M + 1$-th
largest eigenvalue of
$\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}$
by $c_{\kappa}$ and ${\overset{\sim}{\lambda}}_{M + 1}$ respectively.
:::

::: {#Thmthm2 .ltx_theorem .ltx_theorem_thm}
###### [[Theorem 2]{#Thmthm2.1.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold}]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_theorem}[ ]{#Thmthm2.2.2 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold}(Error bound of Nyström approximation)[.]{#Thmthm2.3.3 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold} {#theorem-2-error-bound-of-nyström-approximation. .ltx_title .ltx_runin .ltx_title_theorem}

::: {#Thmthm2.p1 .ltx_para}
[With probability at least $1 - \delta$, it holds that:]{#Thmthm2.p1.1.1
.ltx_text .ltx_font_italic}

  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     ${\mathcal{E}^{\prime} \leq {{\overset{\sim}{\lambda}}_{M + 1} + {\frac{N\operatorname{}C}{\sqrt{M}}\operatorname{}c_{\kappa}\operatorname{}\left( {2 + {\log\frac{1}{\delta}}} \right)}}}.$   
  -- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
:::
:::

::: {#S4.p4 .ltx_para}
Plugging this back to $\mathcal{E}$, we arrive at the corollary below.
:::

::: {#Thmcoro1 .ltx_theorem .ltx_theorem_coro}
###### [[Corollary 1]{#Thmcoro1.1.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold}]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_theorem}[.]{#Thmcoro1.2.2 .ltx_text .ltx_font_bold} {#corollary-1. .ltx_title .ltx_runin .ltx_title_theorem}

::: {#Thmcoro1.p1 .ltx_para}
[With probability at least $1 - \delta$, the following bound
exists:]{#Thmcoro1.p1.1.1 .ltx_text .ltx_font_italic}

  -- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     ${\mathcal{E} \leq {{\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}c_{\Lambda}\operatorname{}\left( {{\overset{\sim}{\lambda}}_{M + 1} + {\frac{N\operatorname{}C}{\sqrt{M}}\operatorname{}c_{\kappa}\operatorname{}\left( {2 + {\log\frac{1}{\delta}}} \right)}} \right)} + \sigma_{0}^{2}}}.$   
  -- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
:::
:::

::: {#S4.p5 .ltx_para}
As desired, the upper bound of $\mathcal{E}$ decreases along with the
growing of the number of MC samples $M$.
:::
:::

ELLA (or LLA) finds a GP posterior, so predicts with a variant of the
aforementioned posterior predictive, formulated as
${{p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {{\mathbf{x}}_{*},\mathcal{D}} \right.)}} = {{\mathbb{E}}_{p\operatorname{}{({f|\mathcal{D}})}}\operatorname{}p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {f\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{*})}} \right.)}} \approx {{\mathbb{E}}_{q\operatorname{}{(f)}}\operatorname{}p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {f\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{*})}} \right.)}} = {{\mathbb{E}}_{{\mathbf{f}} \sim {\mathcal{N}\operatorname{}{({g_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{*})}},{\kappa_{\text{ELLA}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}}_{*},{\mathbf{x}}_{*})}})}}}\operatorname{}p\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {\mathbf{f}} \right.)}}}.$
In classification tasks, we use $512$ MC samples to approximate the last
expectation and it is cheap.
:::

### [5.2 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}The Overfitting Issue of LLA {#the-overfitting-issue-of-lla .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S5.SS2.p1 .ltx_para}
Reinspecting [[Equation]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [1]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S2.E1 "1 ‣ 2.1 Laplace Approximation and Its Linearized Variant ‣ 2 Background ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}
and ([[6]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E6 "6 ‣ 3.2 Scale Up LLA by Kernel Approximation ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}),
we see, with more training data involved, the covariance in LA, LLA, and
ELLA shrinks and the uncertainty dissipates. However, under ubiquitous
model
misspecification \[[41](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib41){.ltx_ref}\],
*should the uncertainty vanish that fast?* We perform a study with
ResNets \[[18](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib18){.ltx_ref}\]
on CIFAR-10 to seek an answer. Concretely, we randomly subsample $N$
data from the training set of CIFAR-10, and fit ELLA on them. We depict
the test negative log-likelihood (NLL), accuracy, and expected
calibration error
(ECE) \[[17](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib17){.ltx_ref}\]
of the deployed ELLA in [[Figure]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [2]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.F2 "Figure 2 ‣ 5.1 Specification of Hyperparameters ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}
(c) and [[C.2]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A3.SS2 "C.2 Test Accuracy and ECE of ELLA Vary w.r.t. N ‣ Appendix C More Experimental Results ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}.
We also provide the corresponding results of
LLA$^{*}$[^3^[[^3^[3]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_note}We experiment on LLA$^{*}$
here due to its higher efficiency than LLA-KFAC and LLA-Diag. LLA$^{*}$
is the default option in the [Laplace]{#footnote3.1 .ltx_text
.ltx_font_typewriter}
library \[[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref}\].]{.ltx_note_content}]{.ltx_note_outer}]{#footnote3
.ltx_note .ltx_role_footnote} in [[C.3]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A3.SS3 "C.3 Test NLL of LLA* Varies w.r.t. N ‣ Appendix C More Experimental Results ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}.
The *V-shape* NLL curves across settings reflect the *overfitting* issue
of ELLA and LLA$^{*}$ (or more generally, LLA). [[7]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A3.F7 "Figure 7 ‣ C.3 Test NLL of LLA* Varies w.r.t. N ‣ Appendix C More Experimental Results ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}
also shows that tuning the prior precision w.r.t. marginal likelihood
can alleviate the overfitting of LLA$^{*}$ to some extent, which may be
the reason why such an issue has not been reported by previous works.
But also of note that tuning the prior precision cannot fully eliminate
overfitting.
:::

::: {#S5.SS2.p2 .ltx_para}
To address the overfitting issue, we advocate *performing early stop
when fitting ELLA/LLA on big data*. Specifically, when iterating over
the training data to estimate $\mathbf{G}$ (see [[Equation]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [6]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E6 "6 ‣ 3.2 Scale Up LLA by Kernel Approximation ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}),
we continuously record the NLL of the current ELLA posterior on some
validation data, and stop when there is a trend of overfitting. If we
cannot access a validation set easily, we can apply strong data
augmentation to some training data to form a substitute of the
validation set. Compared to tuning the prior, early stopping also helps
reduce the time cost of processing big data (e.g.,
ImageNet \[[8](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib8){.ltx_ref}\]).
:::

### [5.3 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}Illustrative Regression {#illustrative-regression .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S5.SS3.p1 .ltx_para}
We build a regression problem with $N = 16$ samples from
${y = {{\sin{2\operatorname{}x}} + \epsilon}},{\epsilon \sim {\mathcal{N}\operatorname{}{(0,0.2)}}}$
as shown in [[Figure]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [1]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S1.F1 "Figure 1 ‣ 1 Introduction ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}.
The model is an MLP with 3 hidden layers and [tanh]{#S5.SS3.p1.9.1
.ltx_text .ltx_font_typewriter} activations, and we pretrain it by MAP
for $1000$ iterations. For ELLA, we set $M = 16$ and $K = 5$ for
efficiency. Unless stated otherwise, we use the interfaces of
[Laplace]{#S5.SS3.p1.9.2 .ltx_text
.ltx_font_typewriter} \[[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref}\]
to implement LLA, LLA-KFAC, LLA-Diag, and LLA$^{*}$. The hyperparameters
of the competitors are *equivalent* to those of ELLA except for some
dedicated ones like $M$ and $K$. It is clear that ELLA delivers a closer
approximation to LLA than LLA-Diag and LLA$^{*}$. We further quantify
the quality of the predictive distributions produced by these
approximate LLA methods using certain metrics. Considering that in this
case, the predictive distribution for one test datum is a Gaussian
distribution, we use the KL divergence between the Gaussians yielded by
the approximate LLA method and vanilla LLA as a proxy of the
approximation error (averaged over a set of test points). The results
are reported in [[C.4]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A3.SS4 "C.4 Comparison on the Approximation Error to Vanilla LLA ‣ Appendix C More Experimental Results ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}.
As shown, LLA-KFAC comes pretty close to LLA. Yet, LLA seems to
*underestimate* in-between uncertainty in this setting, so ELLA seems to
be a more reliable (instead of more accurate) approximation than
LLA-KFAC. We also highlight the higher scalability of ELLA than LLA-KFAC
(see [[Figure]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [4]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.F4 "Figure 4 ‣ 5.2 The Overfitting Issue of LLA ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}(c)),
which reflects that ELLA can strike a good trade-off between efficacy
and efficiency.
:::
:::

### [5.4 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_subsection}CIFAR-10 Classification {#cifar-10-classification .ltx_title .ltx_title_subsection}

::: {#S5.SS4.p1 .ltx_para}
Then, we evaluate ELLA on CIFAR-10 benchmark using ResNet
architectures \[[18](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib18){.ltx_ref}\].
We obtain pretrained MAP models from the open source community. Apart
from MAP, LLA$^{*}$, LLA-Diag, LLA-KFAC, we further introduce last-layer
LLA with KFAC approximation (LLA$^{*}$-KFAC) and mean-field VI via
Bayesian
finetuning \[[10](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib10){.ltx_ref}\]
(MFVI-BF)[^4^[[^4^[4]{.ltx_tag
.ltx_tag_note}Flipout \[[56](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib56){.ltx_ref}\]
trick is deployed for variance-reduced gradient
estimation.]{.ltx_note_content}]{.ltx_note_outer}]{#footnote4 .ltx_note
.ltx_role_footnote} as baselines. LLA cannot be directly applied as the
GGN matrices are huge. These methods all locate in the family of
Gaussian approximate posteriors and are all *post-hoc* applied to the
pretrained models, so the comparisons will be fair. Regarding the
setups, we use $M = 2000$ and $K = 20$ for ELLA;[^5^[[^5^[5]{.ltx_tag
.ltx_tag_note}Storing $2000$ vectors of size $P$ is costly, so we trade
time for memory and compute them whenever
needed.]{.ltx_note_content}]{.ltx_note_outer}]{#footnote5 .ltx_note
.ltx_role_footnote} we use $20$ MC samples to estimate the posterior
predictive of MFVI-BF (as it incurs $20$ NN forward passes), and use
$512$ ones for the other methods as stated. We have enabled *the tuning
of the prior precision* for all LLA baselines, but not for ELLA.
:::

::: {#S5.SS4.p2 .ltx_para}
We present the comparison on test accuracy, NLL, and ECE in
[[Table]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [1]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.T1 "Table 1 ‣ 5.2 The Overfitting Issue of LLA ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}.
As shown, ELLA exhibits superior NLL and ECE across settings. MFVI-BF
also gives good NLL. LLA$^{*}$ and LLA$^{*}$-KFAC can improve the
uncertainty and calibration of MAP, yet underperforming ELLA. LLA-Diag
and LLA-KFAC fail for unclear reasons (also reported by
\[[9](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib9){.ltx_ref}\]),
we thus exclude them from the following studies.
:::

::: {#S5.SS4.p3 .ltx_para}
We then examine the models on the widely used out-of-distribution (OOD)
generalization/robustness benchmark CIFAR-10
corruptions \[[19](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib19){.ltx_ref}\]
and report the results in [[Figure]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [3]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.F3 "Figure 3 ‣ 5.2 The Overfitting Issue of LLA ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}
and [[C.5]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#A3.SS5 "C.5 More Results on CIFAR-10 Corruptions ‣ Appendix C More Experimental Results ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}.
It is prominent that ELLA surpasses the baselines in aspects of NLL and
ECE at various levels of skew, implying its ability to make conservative
predictions for OOD inputs.
:::

::: {#S5.SS4.p4 .ltx_para}
We further evaluate the models on the combination of the CIFAR-10 test
data and the OOD SVHN test data. The predictions on SVHN are all
regarded as wrong due to label shift. We plot the average error rate of
the samples with $\leq \tau$ ($\tau \in {\lbrack 0,1\rbrack}$)
confidence in [[Figure]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [4]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.F4 "Figure 4 ‣ 5.2 The Overfitting Issue of LLA ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}

(b) displays how $K$ impacts the test NLL. We see that
$K \in {\lbrack 20,30\rbrack}$ can already lead to good performance,

it, we can actively control the performance vs. cost trade-off.
[[Figure]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag} [4]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.F4 "Figure 4 ‣ 5.2 The Overfitting Issue of LLA ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}
(c) shows the comparison on the time used for predicting all CIFAR-10

## [6 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_section}Related Work {#related-work .ltx_title .ltx_title_section}

::: {#S6.p1 .ltx_para}
LA \[[38](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib38){.ltx_ref},
[50](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib50){.ltx_ref},
[14](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib14){.ltx_ref},
[30](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib30){.ltx_ref},
[22](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib22){.ltx_ref},
[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref},
[7](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib7){.ltx_ref}\]
locally approximates the Bayesian posterior with a Gaussian
distribution, analogous to VI with Gaussian
variationals \[[16](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib16){.ltx_ref},
[1](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib1){.ltx_ref},
[36](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib36){.ltx_ref},
[52](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib52){.ltx_ref},
[46](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib46){.ltx_ref}\]
and
SWAG \[[39](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib39){.ltx_ref}\].
LA can be applied to pretrained models effortlessly while the acquired
posteriors are potentially restrictive (see [[Section]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [5.5]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.SS5 "5.5 ImageNet Classification ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}).
VI enjoys higher flexibility yet relies on costly training;
BayesAdapter \[[10](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib10){.ltx_ref}\]
seems to be a remedy to the issue but its accessibility is still lower
than LA. SWAG stores a series of SGD iterates to heuristically construct
an approximate posterior and is empirically weaker than
LA/LLA \[[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref}\].
:::

::: {#S6.p2 .ltx_para}
Though more expressive approaches like deep
ensembles \[[32](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib32){.ltx_ref}\]
and
MCMC \[[55](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib55){.ltx_ref},
[4](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib4){.ltx_ref},
[63](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib63){.ltx_ref}\]
can explicitly explore diverse posterior modes, they face limitations in
efficiency and scalability. What's more, it has been shown that LA/LLA
can perform on par with or better than deep ensembles and cyclical MCMC
on multiple
benchmarks \[[6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib6){.ltx_ref}\].
This may be attributed to the un-identified, complicated relationships
between the parameters space and the function space of
DNNs \[[59](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib59){.ltx_ref}\].
Also of note that LA can embrace deep ensembles to capture multiple
posterior
modes \[[13](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib13){.ltx_ref}\].
:::

::: {#S6.p3 .ltx_para}
\[[9](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib9){.ltx_ref}\]
introduces a general kernel approximation technique using neural
networks. By contrast, we focus on leveraging kernel approximation to
accelerate Laplace approximation. Thus, the focus of the two works is
distinct. Indeed, this work shares a similar idea with the Sec 4.3 in
\[[9](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib9){.ltx_ref}\]
that LLA can be accelerated by kernel approximation. But, except for
such an idea, this work differentiates from
\[[9](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib9){.ltx_ref}\]
in aspects like motivations, techniques, implementations, theoretical
backgrounds, and applications. Our implementation, theoretical analysis,
and some empirical findings are all novel.
:::
:::

::: {#S7 .section .ltx_section}
## [7 ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_section}Conclustion {#conclustion .ltx_title .ltx_title_section}

::: {#S7.p1 .ltx_para}
This paper proposes ELLA, a simple, effective, and reliable approach for
Bayesian deep learning. ELLA addresses the unreliability issues of
existing approximations to LLA and is implemented based on Nyström
method. We offer theoretical guarantees for ELLA and perform extensive
studies to testify its efficacy and scalability. ELLA currently accounts
for only the predictive, and extending it to estimate model evidence for
model
selection \[[38](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib38){.ltx_ref}\]
deserves future investigation. Using model evidence to select the number
of remaining eigenpairs $K$ for ELLA is also viable.
:::
:::

###### Proof. {#proof. .ltx_title .ltx_runin .ltx_font_italic .ltx_title_proof}

::: {#A1.SS4.1.p1 .ltx_para}
With
$\mathbf{K} = {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}} \succ 0$,
let
$\mathbf{P} = {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{K}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}}$.
By Woodbury matrix identity,

  -- ---------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     $\mathbf{\Sigma}^{\prime}$   $= {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}} + {{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}} \right\rbrack^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 
                                  $= {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}} + {\mathbf{K}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}} \right\rbrack^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               
                                  $= {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {\mathbf{K}^{- 1} - {\mathbf{K}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{- 1}/\sigma_{0}^{2}} + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{K}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}})}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{K}^{- 1}}} \right\rbrack\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}}$   
                                  ${= {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {\mathbf{P} - {{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{- 1}/\sigma_{0}^{2}} + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}})}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}}} \right\rbrack}}.$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 
  -- ---------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --

It is interesting to see that $\mathbf{P}$ is a projector:
$\mathbf{P}^{2} = \mathbf{P}$ and
${\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top} = \mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}$.
Then

  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     ${\mathbf{\Sigma}^{\prime} = {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {\mathbf{P} - {{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\left( {\left. \mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{- 1}/\sigma_{0}^{2} \right. + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}} \right)^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}}} \right\rbrack}}.$   
  -- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --

By Woodbury matrix identity again,

  -- ---------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     $\mathbf{\Sigma}^{\prime}$   $= \sigma_{0}^{2}\left\lbrack \mathbf{P} - \sigma_{0}^{2}{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\left( \mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}} - \mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P}{(\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}} \right. \right.$                                                                                                                
                                  $\left. \left. {+ {\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P})}^{- 1}{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}} \right)\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P} \right\rbrack$                                                                      
                                  $= \sigma_{0}^{2}\left\lbrack \mathbf{P} - \sigma_{0}^{2}\left( {\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P} - {\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P}{(\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}} \right. \right.$   
                                  $\left. \left. {+ {\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P})}^{- 1}{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P} \right) \right\rbrack.$                                                                     
  -- ---------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --

.ltx_inline-block .ltx_markedasmath .ltx_transformed_outer

[${{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} = {\mathbf{T}^{- 1} - {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}}$]{#A1.SS4.1.p1.7.m2.1.1.1.1
.ltx_p} ]{.ltx_transformed_inner

.ltx_inline-block .ltx_markedasmath .ltx_transformed_outer
style="width:102.5pt;height:12.1pt;vertical-align:-3.8pt;"}. It follows
that

  -- ---------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     $\mathbf{\Sigma}^{\prime}$   ${= {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\left\lbrack {\mathbf{P} - {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{T}^{- 1} - {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}} - {{({\mathbf{T}^{- 1} - {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}})}\operatorname{}\mathbf{T}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{T}^{- 1} - {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}})}}} \right)}} \right\rbrack} = {\mathbf{T} + {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{I}_{P}})}}}}.$   
  -- ---------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --

As a result,

  -- --------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     $\mathcal{E}$   $= {\|{\mathbf{\Sigma}^{\prime} - \mathbf{\Sigma}}\|}$                                                               
                     $= {\|{{\mathbf{T} + {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{I}_{P}})}}} - \mathbf{\Sigma}}\|}$        
                     $\leq {{\|{\mathbf{T} - \mathbf{\Sigma}}\|} + {\sigma_{0}^{2}\operatorname{}{\|{\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{I}_{P}}\|}}}$   
                     ${= {{\|{\mathbf{T} - \mathbf{\Sigma}}\|} + \sigma_{0}^{2}}},$                                                       
  -- --------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --

where we leverage the fact that the eigenvalue of the projector
$\mathbf{P}$ is either $1$ or $0$. And

  -- --------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     $\left\| {\mathbf{T} - \mathbf{\Sigma}} \right\|$   $= {\|{- {\mathbf{T}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{T}^{- 1} - \mathbf{\Sigma}^{- 1}})}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Sigma}}}\|}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                         $= {\|{- {\mathbf{T}\operatorname{}{({{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}})}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Sigma}}}\|}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
                                                         $\leq {{\|\mathbf{T}\|}\operatorname{}{\|\mathbf{\Sigma}\|}\operatorname{}{\|{{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}}\|}}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             
                                                         $\leq \frac{\|{{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}}\|}{\lambda_{\text{min}}\operatorname{}{({{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} + {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}})}\operatorname{}\lambda_{\text{min}}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}} + {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}})}}$   
                                                         ${\leq {\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|{{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}}\|}}},$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                
  -- --------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --

where $\lambda_{\text{min}}\operatorname{}{( \cdot )}$ denotes the
smallest eigenvalue of a matrix. The last inequality holds due to that
the eigenvalues of
${{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} + {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}$
and
${\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}} + {\mathbf{I}_{P}/\sigma_{0}^{2}}$
are larger than or equal to $1/\sigma_{0}^{2}$ as
${\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}$
and
$\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}$
are SPSD. To estimate the upper bound of
$\|{\mathbf{T} - \mathbf{\Sigma}}\|$, we lay out the following Lemma.

::: {#A1.SS4.2.p2 .ltx_para}
It follows that

  -- -------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
     $\left\| {\mathbf{T} - \mathbf{\Sigma}} \right\| \leq$   $\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|{{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}}\|}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         
     $=$                                                      $\frac{\sigma_{0}^{4}}{2} \parallel {{({{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}} + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}}})}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}})}}$                                                               
                                                              $+ {({\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}} - \mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}})}{(\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\mathbf{P} + \mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}})} \parallel$                                                                                                                                                                                              
     $\leq$                                                   $\frac{\sigma_{0}^{4}}{2}\operatorname{}{\|{{({{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}} + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}}})}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}})}}\|}$                                                     
                                                              $+ {\frac{\sigma_{0}^{4}}{2}\operatorname{}{\|{({{({{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}} + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}}})}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}})}})}^{\top}\|}}$                                      
     $=$                                                      $\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|{{({{{\mathbf{P}\mathbf{J}}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}} + {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}}})}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{P}} - {\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}^{\frac{1}{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}}})}}\|}$                                                               [(${\|\mathbf{A}^{\top}\|} = {\|\mathbf{A}\|}$)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
     $=$                                                      $\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|{{({\mathbf{P} + \mathbf{I}_{P}})}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{I}_{P}})}}\|}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
     $=$                                                      $\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{I}_{P}})}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{P} + \mathbf{I}_{P}})}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}\|}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  [(Lemma [[1]{.ltx_text .ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#Thmlemm1 "Lemma 1 (Proposition of Weinstein–Aronszajn identity). ‣ Proof. ‣ A.4 Proof of Theorem 1 ‣ Appendix A Proof ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref})]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
     $=$                                                      $\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{P} - \mathbf{I}_{P}})}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}\|}$                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  [($\mathbf{P}^{2} = \mathbf{P}$)]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_equation .ltx_align_right}
     $=$                                                      $\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|{\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}})}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}})}}\|}$         
     $\leq$                                                   ${\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}{\|\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\|}\operatorname{}{\|{{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}})}^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}}\|}}.$      
  -- -------------------------------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -- -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

It is easy to show that
${\|\mathbf{\Lambda}_{\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}}\|} \leq c_{\Lambda}$ with
$c_{\Lambda}$ as a finite constant. For example, when handling
regression tasks,
$- {{\log p}\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {\mathbf{g}} \right.)}}$
typically boils down to
${\frac{1}{2\operatorname{}\sigma_{\text{noise}}^{2}}\operatorname{}{({{\mathbf{g}} - {\mathbf{y}}})}}\top{({{\mathbf{g}} - {\mathbf{y}}})}$
where $\sigma_{\text{noise}}^{2}$ refers to the variance of data noise,
so
${- {{{\nabla_{{\mathbf{g}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{g}}}^{2}\log}p}\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {\mathbf{g}} \right.)}}} = {\frac{1}{\sigma_{\text{noise}}^{2}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{I}_{C}}$
and $c_{\Lambda} = \frac{1}{\sigma_{\text{noise}}^{2}}$. When facing
classification cases,
$- {{\log p}\operatorname{}{(\left. {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {\mathbf{g}} \right.)}}$
becomes the softmax cross-entropy loss. It is guaranteed that
$c_{\Lambda} \leq 2$ because

  -- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --
     $\parallel - \nabla_{{\mathbf{g}}\operatorname{}{\mathbf{g}}}^{2}\log p\left( {\mathbf{y}} \middle| {\mathbf{g}} \right) \parallel =$   $\|{{\operatorname{diag}{({{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}})}} - {{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}\operatorname{}{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}^{\top}}}\|$         
     $\leq$                                                                                                                                  ${\|{\operatorname{diag}{({{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}})}}\|} + {\|{{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}\operatorname{}{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}^{\top}}\|}$   
     $\leq$                                                                                                                                  ${{1 + {\|{{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}^{\top}\operatorname{}{softmax}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{g}})}}\|}} \leq {1 + 1} = 2}.$                                                     
  -- --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- --

To summarize, we have the following bound for the approximation error
$\mathcal{E}$:

  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ --
     ${\mathcal{E} \leq {\left\| {\mathbf{T} - \mathbf{\Sigma}} \right\| + \sigma_{0}^{2}} = {{\sigma_{0}^{4}\operatorname{}c_{\Lambda}\operatorname{}\left\| {{\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\left( {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}} \right)^{- 1}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}} - {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\mathbf{X}}^{\top}}} \right\|} + \sigma_{0}^{2}}}.$   
  -- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ --

∎

## [Appendix B ]{.ltx_tag .ltx_tag_appendix}How Does ELLA Address the Scalability Issues of Nyström Approximation {#appendix-b-how-does-ella-address-the-scalability-issues-of-nyström-approximation .ltx_title .ltx_title_appendix}

::: {#A2.p1 .ltx_para}
Existing works like
NeuralEF \[[9](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#bib.bib9){.ltx_ref}\]
highlight the scalability issues of the Nyström approximation when
applied to NTKs, but they have been successfully addressed by ELLA,
which reflects our technical novelty. On the one hand, the cost of
eigendecomposing the $M \times M$ matrix in our case is low considering
that we set $M < 10^{4}$. As shown in [[Figure]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [2]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S5.F2 "Figure 2 ‣ 5.1 Specification of Hyperparameters ‣ 5 Experiments ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref}(a)(b),
$M = 2000$ can lead to reasonably small approximation errors for both
the Nyström approximation and the resulting ELLA covariance. On the
other hand, the prediction cost at test time is reduced by two
innovations in this work. Firstly, looking at [[Equation]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag} [14]{.ltx_text
.ltx_ref_tag}](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2210.12642#S3.E14 "14 ‣ 3.3 Approximate NTKs via the Nyström Method ‣ 3 Methodology ‣ Accelerated Linearized Laplace Approximation for Bayesian Deep Learning"){.ltx_ref},
typical Nyström method first computes
$J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}\operatorname{}\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}$
(i.e., evaluate the NTK similarities between $({\mathbf{x}},i)$ and all
training data $\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}$) and then multiplies the
result with $\mathbf{u}_{k} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{M}$ to get the evaluation
of $k$-th eigenfunction. So it needs to store
$\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}} \in {\mathbb{R}}^{M \times P}$
and compute $M$ Jacobian-vector products (JVPs). But this work proposes
to first compute
${\mathbf{v}_{k} = {\mathbf{J}_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}},\overset{\sim}{\mathbf{X}}}^{\top}\operatorname{}\mathbf{u}_{k}}},{k = {1,\ldots,K}}$
(we omit the scalar multiplier) and then obtain the evaluation of $k$-th
eigenfunction by
$J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}\operatorname{}\mathbf{v}_{k}$.
Namely, we only need to store $K$ vectors of size $P$ and compute $K$
JVPs. In most cases, $M = 2000$ and $K = 20$, so the benefits of our
technique are obvious. Secondly, we leverage forward-mode autodiff
(fwAD) to efficiently compute the JVPs where we do not need to
explicitly compute and store the Jacobian
$J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}$ but to invoke
once fwAD. Further, fwAD enables the parallel evaluation of JVPs over
all output dimensions, i.e., we can concurrently compute
${{{J_{\hat{\mathbf{θ}}}\operatorname{}{({\mathbf{x}},i)}\operatorname{}\mathbf{v}_{k}},i} = 1},{\ldots,C}$
in one forward pass. These techniques make the evaluation of ELLA equal
to performing $K$ forward passes under the scope of fwAD. This is
similar to other BNNs that perform $S$ forward passes with different MC
parameter samples to estimate the posterior predictive.
:::
:::
